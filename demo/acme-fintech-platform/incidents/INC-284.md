# INC-284: OAuth provider outage forces enterprise logouts

- **Severity:** SEV-1
- **Date:** 2025-03-18
- **Duration:** 17 minutes
- **Author:** Dana Whitfield (SRE on-call)
- **Related:** ADR-012, PR #842

## Summary

Between 14:02 and 14:19 UTC, Google and Azure OAuth endpoints returned elevated
5xx errors. Because `authenticateUser()` verified every request against the
identity provider, active enterprise users were force-logged-out. One customer
lost an in-progress payment.

## Impact

- ~3,400 enterprise users logged out mid-session.
- 1 failed payment flow (later recovered manually).
- 6 support escalations; 2 executive escalations.

## Root cause

A hard runtime dependency on the OAuth provider for *already-authenticated*
sessions. There was no cached session to fall back to, and no retry/backoff around
the provider call.

## Resolution

Short term: provider recovered on its own; we manually extended sessions.

Long term (tracked in ADR-012, shipped in PR #842):
- Cache sessions in Redis and validate against the cache first.
- Retry the provider with bounded backoff, then fall back to the cached session.
- Rotate refresh tokens to remove the replay risk introduced by longer-lived sessions.
